What you're looking at

This is the new Home. It used to be a flat list of session cards. Now it's a feed: session cards + auto-generated milestone cards, with a pinned FINA Points tile at the top.

1 Pinned FINA Points tile

This is the headline — one number that captures everything. 562 means roughly "solid age-group level." The arrow shows month-over-month delta.

FINA Points is a 0–1000 universal score used by serious coaches worldwide. It already adjusts for stroke, distance, gender, and pool type — so parents only have to track one number. See swim-domain-research.md for the full background.

900–1000World / Olympic finalist
700–900National-level junior
500–700Strong regional age-grouper
300–500Developing competitor

2 Session cards now show points

Each session card shows its highest-scoring swim in FINA Points, so you can see at a glance "Wednesday was a good day, Monday was a recovery day." The drill count and duration are still there.

3 PB tags inline

If a session contained a personal best, the card carries a small "🏆 PB!" tag with the delta. Tapping the card opens the session for the full drill detail.

4 Milestone cards (auto-generated)

These are not sessions — they're auto-generated celebrations. Streak cards, volume cards, "biggest improvement" cards. They're anchored to the day the milestone was achieved, so the feed stays honest chronologically.

Rule of thumb: at most one milestone card per day. They're priority-ranked (PB > big delta > streak > distance) so we never have a "wall of confetti."

5 Empty / cold-start state (not pictured)

For a new user with 0–2 sessions, the feed shows a "Log 3 sessions to start seeing your FINA trend" placeholder instead of an empty wall. After session 3, the tile activates.

Analytics tab — the "study" room

Where parents go once a week (or coaches go after every session). Replaces the underused Explore tab. Three sections by default, with an "Advanced" toggle for coach-grade stuff.

1 FINA Points trend

The single most important chart. One line: best FINA score per week. Filled area underneath for visual weight. The dot on the right is the most recent week.

Below the chart: average, best, and the cumulative delta. "▲ +47 since week 1" is the answer to "are we getting better?" — yes, by 47 FINA Points across 12 weeks. That's substantial.

2 Personal bests table

One row per event the swimmer has done this season. PBs from the last 7 days carry a green "PB this week" tag.

Tapping a row would (P3+) open the per-event detail with a time-series chart for that specific (stroke × distance).

3 Stroke balance

What percentage of training volume was spent on each stroke. Coaches care about this a lot — kids under 14 should have roughly even distribution. A weak fly (only 18% here) hints at an avoidance pattern.

The bars are colour-coded the same as the stroke pills in DrillSheet, for visual consistency.

4 Advanced toggle (collapsed by default)

Behind this sit coach-grade metrics: Critical Swim Speed (the pace you can sustain indefinitely, derived from any timed 200 + 400 effort), improvement velocity (slope of best times), and eventually age-group standards context ("you're 1.2s off the AA cut").

Parents shouldn't have to learn what CSS is. Coaches will spot it immediately.

5 What's deliberately missing

No leaderboard, no peer comparison, no goal forecasting. All deferred to v2+. We can ship Analytics with just these three sections and a working FINA Points calculator.

One-time onboarding

FINA Points needs gender and pool type to be accurate. Birth year is for future age-group features (P5+). This flow appears once, after first sign-in — or when the user first taps the FINA tile and we realise we don't have these.

1 Why we ask

Without gender, we can't pick the right FINA base-time table — male and female world records are different, so the scoring scale differs. Without pool type, a 50 free in a 25m pool gets scored against the wrong world record (the 50m-pool one).

Birth year is optional v1 (FINA Points doesn't need it), but we collect it now so age-group features in v2 don't require a second onboarding.

2 Friction budget

Three fields, pre-filled where we can guess (default pool guess based on locale). Should take ~10 seconds. Anything more and we lose users at the front door.

3 What if they skip it?

The FINA tile on Home shows "Set up to see your score" with a tap-to-complete CTA. Sessions still log normally; just no points until the profile is filled.

The PB toast — the dopamine hit

This is the smallest, highest-leverage piece of the whole feature. Fires at drill-save time when the new time beats the previous PB for that (stroke × distance). Independent of the Analytics tab.

1 Where it appears

Modal/banner on top of the session screen for ~4 seconds, dismissable. Confetti animation optional but encouraged. Reactions matter — this is the moment that hooks parents on opening the app.

2 The information stack

Three layers, biggest to smallest:

  1. Headline emotion — "🏆 New PB!"
  2. The fact — "50 Free, 36.20, 0.40s faster"
  3. The context — "575 FINA Points, +28 vs prior" (educates parents about FINA without them noticing)

3 Edge cases

If 5 PBs happen at once (e.g. backfilling a meet result), one combined card with a list. Never five toasts queued.

For non-FINA-scoreable distances (e.g. 25m sprints), still show the PB toast — just omit the FINA context line.

4 Anti-pattern: don't over-fire

Beating your own PB by 0.01s every other session would be annoying. Threshold: surface a toast only when delta > 0.05s OR it's the first PB ever for that event.
